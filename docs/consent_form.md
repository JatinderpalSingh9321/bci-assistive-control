# Informed Consent Form for BCI Experiment

## Project Information
**Project Title:** Non-Invasive BCI for Assistive Control
**Researchers:** Group No. 7
**Affiliation:** 8th Semester Major Project

## Study Purpose
The purpose of this study is to collect EEG (electroencephalogram) signals while you perform motor imagery tasks (imagining moving your left or right hand). This data will be used to train an Artificial Intelligence model to recognize these mental commands, which corresponds to Phase 2 of our project.

## Procedures
If you agree to participate, the following will happen:
1. Conductive gel will be applied to your scalp, and electrodes will be attached using the 10-20 system.
2. You will be seated in a comfortable chair facing a computer screen.
3. You will be asked to follow visual cues (arrows pointing LEFT or RIGHT) and imagine squeezing the corresponding fist without actually moving your arms.
4. The entire recording session will take approximately 30 minutes, consisting of short 3-minute blocks with rest periods in between.

## Risks and Discomforts
This is a non-invasive procedure. The Upside Down Labs BioAmp EXG Pill kit uses completely safe, isolated physiological signal recording. You might experience minor discomfort from the electrode gel, which can be easily washed off with water after the session. There are no known risks associated with participating in this study.

## Voluntary Participation
Your participation is completely voluntary. You may withdraw from the study at any time for any reason without penalty.

## Confidentiality
All data collected during this study will be anonymized. Your name and identifying information will not be associated with the recorded EEG signals. The anonymous data may be published or shared for academic purposes.
